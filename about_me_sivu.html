My hobbies

Recording

The trombone is not the most popular instrument in the world, which is evident when we compare the amount of recordings for the trombone and for the piano for example. Nevertheless, the lack of recordings has made me eager to make recordings of my own. It's quite fun to make your own compositions or arrangements and try to record them on tape in satisfatory manner. Mostly, I have done arrangements for trombone trio and trombone quartet. After a recording session it's nice to listen the results and use finished recordings in other projects. On recording sessons I use the following equipment: Audacity software, AKG D40 dynamic instrument microphone, JBL Tune750BT headphones and Focusrite Scarlett sound card. Playing the Trombone

Making music on spare time is one of the best things I know. One of the greatest things in trombone playing is ensemble playing, especially in a symphony orchestra, in a trombone quartet or in a big band. I love how colourfully and versatile way a trombone section can sound. I mainly play classical music since I've grown up listening to that genre but I would like to learn more jazz or funk trombone techniques also later in life. Like many other trombonists, I prefer the tenor trombone as my first choise of instrument but occationally I like to play the bass trombone in big bands and the alto trombone in symphony orchestras.

Thaiboxing

Thaiboxing, also known as Muay Thai, is another important hobby for me. It helps me to forget my worries of everyday life and physically keeps me in shape. Thaiboxing is a very versatile sport where one commonly uses techniques like: boxing (a little bit like the western boxers do), different kinds of kicks (high, low, roundhouse kicks and so on), elbows, clinching, knees and sweeps. Because of these multiple array of techniques, the sport is often referred to be 'the art of eight limbs'. I warmly recommend to try out thaiboxing if you would like to improve your balance, health, muscles or just for having some fun. As an amateur, thaiboxing is very enjoyable especially when you have the same mind set as your training partners. One can always find new challenges in the sport. Self-improvement never stops.
